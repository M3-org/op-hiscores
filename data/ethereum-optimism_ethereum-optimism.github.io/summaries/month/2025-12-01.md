## üêõ Issues & Triage
The month was characterized by intensive triage and discussion around numerous high-priority issues, reflecting a focus on hardening the core protocol.

- **Closed Issues:** A single documentation issue regarding the consensus layer sync path was closed due to a lack of context ([#1895](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/issues/1895)).

- **New & Active Issues:** Discussions centered on several critical areas:
    - **Node Stability & Synchronization:** A significant portion of the month's effort was spent on node-related issues. These included an `op-node` invalid block signature error ([#17448]), nodes getting stuck in EL sync mode ([#18468]), and nodes failing to sync with "missing trie node" errors ([#18581]). Flaky P2P connections between `op-geth` and `op-reth` nodes in system tests were also identified as a high-priority problem ([#18570]). Additionally, a long-standing issue concerning `proxyd`'s potential to serve stale blocks was slated for attention in the new year ([#501]).
    - **Fault Proofs & Security:** Security of the fault proof system was a key concern. A critical fuzzing test failure in `OptimismPortal2.depositTransaction` when handling a null address was identified and prioritized for an immediate fix ([#18554]). Another discussion focused on an `op-challenger` panic caused by a `nil pointer dereference` when interacting with an L1 client not upgraded for Dencun, highlighting compatibility challenges ([#18608]).
    - **Protocol Optimization & Upgrades:** Conversations continued on simplifying the batcher by removing legacy `isPectra` checks ([#18479]) and the need to offload expensive operations in `kona-sp1` to improve ZK proof performance ([#18533]). The finalization of Upgrade 18 releases was also tracked, pending governance approval and testnet validation ([#18548]).
    - **Ecosystem Expansion:** New pull requests were opened to add the Canonical SKL Deployment from Ethereum to Base ([#1242](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/pull/1242)) and the AMIE Token on World Chain Sepolia ([#1243](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/pull/1243)).
