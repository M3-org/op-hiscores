## üêõ Issues & Triage
No new issues were opened and no existing issues were closed this week. However, discussions on several active issues pointed toward significant future work on protocol security and efficiency.

*   **New & Active Issues:**
    *   **`proxyd` Backend Synchronization ([#501](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/issues/501)):** A critical discussion is underway to prevent `proxyd` from returning outdated block data when backends are out of sync, a phenomenon described as "backwards time travel." Proposed solutions include fanning out `eth_call` requests to all backends or making `proxyd` aware of "block out of range" errors to silently fall back to a healthy backend.
    *   **`op-batcher` Blob Priority Fee Tracking ([#18102](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/issues/18102)):** A proposal was made to improve how the batcher handles transaction fees during busy periods. The suggestion involves having the starting priority fee for a new transaction "decay" from the previous one, rather than resetting to a minimum. This is seen as a potential "quick win" to ensure transaction inclusion while a more comprehensive fee tracker is considered.
    *   **Acceptance Test Enhancements ([#16450](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/issues/16450)):** An issue was highlighted where the `sysgo` orchestrator lacks awareness of flashblocks and rbuilders, preventing related tests from being run. Implementing this is desired to validate changes in `kona-node` and `rollup-boost`.
    *   **`migrate` Method Scope ([#17743](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/ethereum-optimism.github.io/issues/17743)):** Discussions clarified that this issue is now scoped specifically to OPCM changes, with related devstack work being tracked separately, paving the way for its eventual resolution.
