## üêõ Issues & Triage
Issue management this week centered on improving Kona client compatibility and planning for future protocol upgrades and tooling changes.

- **Closed Issues:**
    - **Kona & `op-program` Improvements:** Two issues were closed, enhancing client compatibility. Issue [#18203](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/specs/issues/18203) was resolved after an update to Kona fixed its incompatibility with websocket URLs for L2 EL connections. Issue [#17416](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/specs/issues/17416) was closed, as Kona's direct storage lookup for block headers provides a hint similar to the one requested for `op-program`.

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - No new issues were opened this week.
    - **Dispute Game v2 Compatibility:** Discussion continued in [#1148](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/specs/issues/1148) on updating scripts for Dispute Game v2. The focus is on adapting scripts to read arguments from the Dispute Game Factory (DGF) and accounting for multiple permissionless game types.
    - **Kona Custom Prestes:** Progress was noted on [#18271](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/specs/issues/18271), which tracks adding support for embedded custom prestates to Kona, with a fix currently under review.
    - **Tooling Deprecation:** A comment on issue [#16704](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/specs/issues/16704) indicated that Kurtosis is being deprecated for `op-sync-tester` and `op-devstack`, signaling a strategic shift in the project's testing and deployment tooling.
