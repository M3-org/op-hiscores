Work this week centered on preparing for and responding to the Jovian hard fork.

- **New Initialization Work:** A new pull request titled "init" ([#733](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/pull/733)) was opened, though its specific purpose and progress are still pending review.

## üêõ Issues & Triage
Issue management this week was dominated by node synchronization problems, ranging from resolved individual cases to a new, critical-level incident.

- **Closed Issues:**
    - **Node Synchronization:** An issue where an `op-node` failed to synchronize was resolved after the user upgraded and resynced their `op-geth` client, as recommended in the issue discussion ([#18510](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18510)).
    - **Documentation:** Two documentation-related issues were closed, restoring the availability of environment variables in the docs ([#1874](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/1874)) and updating the JSON-RPC API documentation ([#1773](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/1773)).

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - **Critical Sync Failures:** A high-priority issue ([#18581](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18581)) was reported concerning widespread node synchronization failures. Users across multiple chains, including Base, Optimism, and Unichain, are encountering "missing trie node" and "Failed to build payload" errors, particularly when syncing from snapshots. This is a potential blocker for node operators, and maintainers are actively gathering diagnostic information to investigate.
    - **Ongoing Refactoring:** Discussion continues on removing the `isPectra` flag from the batcher ([#18479](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18479)), with a contributor expressing interest in taking on the task.
