## üêõ Issues & Triage
Discussions this week were dominated by active issues concerning protocol architecture, testing, and node stability.

- **Closed Issues:**
  - A documentation issue ([#18095](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18095)) was closed, ensuring the `cannonKonaPrestate` requirement for upgrades is now properly documented.

- **New & Active Issues:**
  - **Interop Architecture Debate ([#18574](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18574)):** A significant discussion is underway regarding the handling of injected replacement transactions for proofs. There is a consensus forming to simplify the protocol by removing an additional deposit transaction, though concerns were raised by `ajsutton` about the potential security implications for challengers. The next steps involve architectural refactoring and updating the Interop specification.
  - **Supernode Proof Testing ([#18621](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18621)):** A conversation has started on how to rework proof action testing to better align with the Supernode architecture. The goal is to make tests more accurately reflect the production environment where `op-challenger` relies on Supernode for data.
  - **Node Synchronization Support ([#18581](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18581)):** A user reported "missing trie node" errors, prompting active troubleshooting from the team to diagnose the configuration and environment issues.
  - **ZK Proof Optimization ([#18533](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-geth/issues/18533)):** The discussion continues on identifying and offloading expensive cryptographic operations like keccak and sha2 to optimize Kona-SP1 performance.
