## üêõ Issues & Triage
Discussions this week centered on architectural planning for the proofs system and smart contract interfaces, with no new or closed issues reported.

- **Closed Issues:** Based on the provided reports, no issues were closed this week.

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - **Architecting the ZK Proof System:** A significant portion of the week's discussion focused on designing the implementation of ZK proofs. Key topics included:
        - **Proof Generation:** The succinct Rust challenger was recommended for generating ZK proofs over extending the Go-based `op-proposer`, signaling a potential shift to a Rust stack for these critical components ([#18319](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-registry/issues/18319)).
        - **Proof Execution:** It was suggested that ZK execution logic should reside in Rust due to `sp1` dependencies, with the possibility of embedding a Rust executor within `vm-runner` ([#18325](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-registry/issues/18325)).
        - **Challenging Games:** A debate is ongoing about whether to reimplement challenger logic in `op-challenger` for the short term or wait for a more integrated long-term Rust solution ([#18322](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-registry/issues/18322)).
    - **Contract Interface Refinements:** A discussion on the `AnchorStateRegistry` proposed expanding the `IDisputeGame` interface. This would remove a direct dependency on `IFaultDisputeGame`, allowing for greater flexibility with different game types while maintaining important sanity checks ([#18347](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-registry/issues/18347)).
