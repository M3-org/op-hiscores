## üêõ Issues & Triage
No issues were closed this week, but several critical discussions progressed on active issues, highlighting future work and potential blockers.

- **Closed Issues:** No issues were closed during this period.

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - **Blockers and Dependencies:** The implementation of the DA Footprint Limit ([#17551](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/17551)) remains blocked, awaiting a required spec derivation.
    - **API Stability:** A significant discussion is ongoing in [#17907](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/17907) regarding `rollupConfig.Genesis.SytemConfig` marshaling. The team is weighing options to avoid breaking zk stacks, debating whether to delay marshaling new fields or update dependent repositories.
    - **Fusaka Upgrade Progress:** Preparations for the Fusaka upgrade are being tracked across multiple issues. The `op-geth` update is complete for Sepolia but still pending for Mainnet ([#682](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/682)). Similarly, the task to update the absolute prestate has been executed on Sepolia, with discussions on how to track the corresponding Mainnet task ([#1206](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/1206)).
    - **Tooling:** A `mise` installation error caused by a version prefix was reported in [#17963](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/17963). Separately, a discussion in [#17942](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/superchain-ops/issues/17942) is exploring whether to enhance `op-up` for local development or if simpler testing methods would suffice.
