## üêõ Issues & Triage
No new issues were opened this week. Triage and discussion focused on resolving existing design challenges and refining testing processes.

- **Closed Issues:**
    - **Testing Strategy Refinement:** An issue regarding the reporting of unused acceptance tests ([#16273](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/design-docs/issues/16273)) was closed. This decision reflects a strategic shift, as the team's primary use of "gateless mode" for testing has made the previous requirement obsolete.

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - **`op-batcher` Fee Management (`#18102`):** A significant discussion continued around improving how the `op-batcher/txmgr` handles blob priority fees. A "quick win" was proposed where the starting fee for a new transaction would "decay" from the previous one, helping to clear backlogs more effectively. This is being weighed against a more comprehensive but larger effort to build a dedicated blob priority fee tracker.
    - **`proxyd` Reliability (`#501`):** A critical discussion highlighted how `proxyd` can experience "backwards time travel" when its backends are out of sync, affecting RPC calls like `eth_call`. Potential solutions were brainstormed, including a "multicall" strategy, silent fallbacks to other backends, and session stickiness, each with different trade-offs.
    - **DA Footprint Limit Documentation (`#1742`):** Discussions continued on the need to document the DA Footprint Limit, with an emphasis on clarifying the risks associated with its configuration and its interaction with the Gas Limit.
