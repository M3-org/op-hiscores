## üêõ Issues & Triage
Issue tracking this month was dominated by important ongoing discussions rather than a high volume of new or closed issues.

- **Closed Issues:**
    - **Chain Upgrade Documentation:** An important issue was closed regarding the documentation of the `cannonKonaPrestate` requirement for standard chain upgrades ([#18095](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/community-hub/issues/18095)). This resolution ensures that the necessary information is available to the documentation team, clarifying a key requirement for ecosystem developers.

- **New & Active Issues:**
    - **Superchain Architecture:** Discussions were active on core Superchain components. A key conversation on [#18574](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/community-hub/issues/18574) explored simplifying the interop specification by removing replacement transactions, noting their limited value in a ZK dispute system. Concurrently, issue [#18621](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/community-hub/issues/18621) addressed the need to adapt proof action testing for the Supernode architecture, emphasizing testing the `op-challenger` against a real Supernode.
    - **Node Operation & Developer Experience:** Challenges with node stability and developer tooling were prominent topics. The discussion in [#18581](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/community-hub/issues/18581) highlighted persistent "missing trie node" errors faced by node operators, prompting questions about the best path forward with OP-GETH or Reth. In parallel, a community-driven solution for fixing project-relative pathing issues in Foundry was confirmed in [#10202](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/community-hub/issues/10202).
