## üêõ Issues & Triage
No issues were closed this week based on the provided data. The focus was on diagnosing new and existing problems, with several critical issues being prioritized.

*   **New & Active Issues:**
    *   **Critical Bugs Identified:** Two high-priority bugs are under active investigation. A `nil pointer dereference` panic in `op-challenger` ([#18608](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18608)) was identified as a serious issue affecting L1 nodes that do not support blob transactions. Additionally, a critical bug in `OptimismPortal2.depositTransaction` ([#18554](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18554)) was discovered through fuzzing, related to incorrect handling of zero-address deposits.
    *   **Node Stability and Performance:** Discussions continued around node synchronization failures ([#18581](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18581)), with evidence pointing to the L1 Execution Client failing to provide necessary state data. Flaky P2P connections between `op-reth` and `op-geth` ([#18570](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18570)) are impacting system test stability. Performance optimization for `kona-sp1` ([#18533](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18533)) is also being tracked, focusing on offloading expensive operations.
    *   **Protocol Design & Release Finalization:** A key architectural discussion is underway regarding the fault-proof system ([#18574](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18574)), weighing the necessity of an injected replacement transaction against potential protocol simplifications. The finalization of Upgrade 18 ([#18548](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/op-analytics/issues/18548)) is being monitored, pending governance approval and successful testnet runs.
