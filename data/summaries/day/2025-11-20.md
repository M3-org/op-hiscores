On Nov 20, 2025, Optimism significantly advanced its dispute game infrastructure with new succinct contracts and `op-geth` upgrades, while also making progress on operational readiness by finalizing a critical runbook for low batcher throughput. A key challenge emerged around the `IFaultDisputeGame` dependency in `AnchorStateRegistry`, sparking active discussions across multiple teams regarding its necessity and implications for future dispute game types.

### Dispute Game Infrastructure & Protocol Enhancements
- **New Succinct Contracts**: Added `op-succinct` contracts, including access control, error/type definitions, external verifier interface, and `OPSuccinctFaultDisputeGame` to enhance dispute game capabilities in `optimism` ([#18277](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/optimism/pull/18277)).

- **Fusaka on L2 Design Finalization**: The design for "fusaka on L2" was finalized and approved in `design-docs` ([#355](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/design-docs/issues/355)).

### Operational Readiness & System Stability

- **Atomic L1 Receipt Parsing**: Implemented atomic parsing of L1 receipts in SystemConfig to prevent node stalling from malformed data in `optimism` ([#18292](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/optimism/pull/18292)).
- **Geth v1.16.4 Upstream Merge**: The tracking issue for the `geth v1.16.4` upstream merge was closed in `optimism` ([#18217](https://github.com/ethereum-optimism/optimism/issues/18217)).
